Why I Started APP Coaching: A Story About Efficiency, Translation, and AI

July 8, 2026

I work for Tradies Success Academy as an Accounting Coach. I'm also quietly launching APP Coaching alongside it. This isn't a full pivot—it's a backup plan, a belief I couldn't ignore, and a solution to a problem I've watched destroy productivity in every organization I've worked for.

Here's why.

The Problem I Kept Seeing

In 2017, I landed at Flight Centre as an Operations Accountant fresh out of my PwC graduate year. I was 25, confident in Excel, and I immediately noticed something that still bothers me: the graduate accountants around me didn't know how to use Excel.

Not advanced Excel. Basic Excel.

They were doing bank reconciliations by hand. Building financial models in a way that would take me 5 minutes to simplify. Wasting hours every single week on tasks that could be automated with a few formulas.

I thought: This can't be it. These are smart people working for a reputable organization, but we're doing data work like it's 1995.

Then I moved to Coronis Group.

Same story. But worse. They were still printing invoices. Literally printing them. The CEO was wet-signing payments. Junior accountants were re-entering data across multiple spreadsheets. Year after year, the same convoluted model. Same inefficiencies. Countless wasted hours.

I worked at Telstra. Aveo Group. YourTown. Every single organization had brilliant people doing financial and operational work in ways that were utterly archaic.

The problem wasn't stupidity. It was this: nobody had taught them better.

How I Got an Unfair Advantage

My PwC graduate year was intense. In audit, you have to be efficient. You learn Excel properly because the alternative is drowning. Functions. Formulas. Automation. The discipline of building a model that's clean, controlled, and scalable.

When I got to Flight Centre, I had a skill most people didn't: I could make data work for me instead of against me.

But here's the thing—that wasn't enough to advance.

What actually moved me up wasn't just efficiency. It was my ability to translate what the data was saying into language leaders could understand. Not jargon. Not complexity. Clarity.

I could look at a messy dataset and turn it into a story. A dashboard that made sense at a glance. A number that answered the actual question someone was asking.

That skill—the ability to take complex financial information and make it obvious—that's what got me recognized. That's what got me into better roles. That's what kept me employed through every company I worked for.

The Catalyst: AI Changed Everything

Fast forward to now. I'm working at Tradies Success Academy as an Accounting Coach. My leader is AI-forward. They're not afraid of Claude. They're not nervous about Gemini. They're asking: What if we automated this? What if we used AI to process this data faster?

And that's when it clicked.

Everything I learned about Excel efficiency? Everything I learned about translating data for decision-makers? AI takes that to another level entirely.

You don't just save hours. You expose insights that were buried. You streamline processes that took weeks. You do in seconds what used to take days.

I started playing with Claude for financial analysis. For workflow automation. For building tools that could actually work alongside human thinking. And I realized: most people have no idea this is possible.

The finance professionals I know? Still drowning in manual processes. Young accountants? Still building Excel models the hard way. Business owners? Still guessing at their numbers because they can't understand their data.

And I'm watching AI solve all of this.

Why I'm Doing This Now

I'm not leaving Tradies Success Academy. I'm not going full-time on APP Coaching tomorrow. This is a soft launch, a backup plan, a realistic approach to something I genuinely believe in.

But I also know this: if I wait for the "right time," if I wait until I'm sure, if I wait until everything is perfect—I'll be watching people struggle with problems I already know how to solve.

So here's what APP Coaching is really about.

It's about closing the gap. The gap between the data you have and the insights you need. The gap between Excel anxiety and Excel confidence. The gap between thinking AI is science fiction and realizing you can build tools with it today.

It's about translation. Taking complex financial or operational information and making it simple enough that decision-makers can actually understand it and act on it.

It's about efficiency. Because once you realize you can save 16 hours per week on a process, or 40% of your accounting team's time on data entry, or build a dashboard that tells you immediately where your business is actually profitable—you can't un-know that.

What I Learned From Every Organization

Every organization I've worked for taught me something. But they all taught me the same lesson in different ways:

Smart people get stuck doing inefficient things because nobody showed them another way.

I got lucky. PwC showed me how to use Excel efficiently. My roles showed me how to translate that for leaders. AI showed me what's possible when you combine both.

Not everyone gets that luck.

So I'm creating it for them.

This is why I started APP Coaching. Not because I'm a guru. Not because I have all the answers. But because I've seen this problem across every organization I've worked for, and I've learned the skills that actually solve it.

And I genuinely believe: once you understand your numbers, once you can build the tools to analyze your business, once you realize AI can help you work smarter—everything changes.

That's what I want to help you build.
